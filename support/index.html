Getting Started

How Ew AI Works

Ew AI uses AI to transform photos of your food into deliberately revolting versions of themselves — a darkly comedic way to make unhealthy eating a little less appealing.

1

Take or select a photo

Tap the photo zone on the home screen. You can snap a fresh shot with your camera or choose an existing image from your photo library. Hit "Retake" anytime if you're not happy with it.

2

Tap "Disgust Me"

Once your food photo is loaded, tap the button to kick off the analysis. Our AI will identify what you're eating, estimate the calories, and craft a uniquely revolting transformation — specific to that food.

3

Face your food

The results screen shows a side-by-side BEFORE / AFTER comparison with the food's real name, its new disgusting name, a calorie count, and a multi-sentence breakdown of everything that makes it revolting.

4

Share the horror

Tap the share button to save or send a composite before/after image — perfect for TikTok, Instagram, or sending to friends who need a reality check about their lunch.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the most common questions about Ew AI.

Why didn't the app recognize my food?

Ew AI uses GPT-4o Vision to analyze your photo, so image quality matters. Make sure the food is well-lit and clearly visible in the frame — avoid blurry, distant, or very dark shots. If the food fills most of the frame, results are significantly better.

The generated image doesn't look very disgusting. Why?

The transformation is specific to the type of food. Meats, fried foods, and processed snacks tend to produce the most extreme results. Fruits and simple whole foods produce subtler changes. Try different foods to find what generates the most horrifying outcomes.

How accurate is the calorie count?

Calorie estimates are based on a typical serving of the identified food. They're meant to be informative, not clinical — treat them as a rough ballpark. For precise nutritional tracking, use a dedicated nutrition app alongside Ew AI.

How long does the analysis take?

Processing usually takes 10–30 seconds depending on your connection. First, the AI analyzes what the food is (a few seconds), then generates the disgusting transformation image (the slower part). You'll see rotating status messages to keep you entertained while you wait.

Why does the app need camera and photo library access?

Camera access lets you photograph food live. Photo library access lets you pick an existing photo. Neither permission is used for anything beyond this — photos are processed for the scan and never stored. You can revoke either permission at any time in iOS Settings.

Can I use Ew AI on my iPad?

Yes — Ew AI supports both iPhone and iPad. The layout scales to larger screens, and the share sheet is adapted for iPad's popover presentation style.

Does Ew AI work offline?

No. Both the food analysis and image generation rely on AI APIs that require an active internet connection. A stable Wi-Fi or cellular connection gives the best experience.

Can I save the result to my camera roll?

Yes. Tap the share button on the results screen and choose "Save Image." This saves a combined BEFORE/AFTER composite image directly to your Photos app.

Troubleshooting

Common Issues

Solutions for the most frequent problems users run into.

The app shows an error after scanning

This is almost always a network issue. Check your internet connection and try again. If the problem persists, restart the app and attempt a new scan. Heavily blurred or very dark photos can occasionally cause the analysis to fail — try a cleaner shot.

Image generation is taking too long

AI image generation is slower on weaker connections. If it's been more than a minute with no result, exit to the home screen and try again with a fresh photo. Moving to a stronger Wi-Fi signal almost always resolves this.

The share button isn't working

Make sure you're on the results screen after a completed scan. On iPad, the share sheet appears as a popover — if it doesn't show, try tapping the button again. If sharing still fails, try saving to your camera roll instead and sharing from the Photos app.

Camera or photo access is denied

If you previously denied the permission prompt, go to Settings → Privacy & Security → Camera (or Photos) → Ew AI and toggle access on. The app requires both permissions to function.

The app crashed or froze

Force-quit the app by swiping it away in the app switcher, then reopen it. If crashes happen repeatedly, try reinstalling the app. If the problem continues, email us with your device model, iOS version, and a description of what you were doing when it crashed.
